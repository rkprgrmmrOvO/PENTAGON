.NET



Hi. I’m Brasco Exequiel M. Polinga, but you can call me Coco. The introduction of .NET was pretty much the most complicated part of web programming I’ve experienced thus far. Amongst all the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript stuff that we had, .NET sticks out like a sore thumb because of how unfamiliar I was to its respective software, Visual Studio. All throughout our first three periods (i.e., preliminary, midterm, and prefinal), we only had to use Visual Studio Code; hence, my lack of knowledge and skill in Visual Studio. It was emotionally and mentally demanding, to say the least, especially for someone who didn’t really sign up to be a programmer as opposed to a visual artist. The only thing that I feel was the most memorable and satisfying during our coverage of .NET was inputting “dotnet run” in our code, which isn’t even found in Visual Studio, or so I’ve recalled. Witnessing our complex codes run in real time was definitely quite a joy, in my opinion. The rest of the .NET stuff, however, I prefer not to touch or comment on. Wrapping my head around this programming stuff was taxing, but it was still very insightful nonetheless.
